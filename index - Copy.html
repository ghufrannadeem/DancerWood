Vision Dancer Wood

Dancer Wood is a family-oriented housing development, aiming to bring the “missing middle” of housing to Saanich. Diversity of housing types and costs is an priority for us all. Saanich itself is demonstrating this by focusing on its District-wide Housing Strategy that will identify areas of challenges and opportunities for our community. Many people have already communicated through past and current engagement activities that providing affordable housing with potential mortgage assistance is a top priority, as is environmentally and socially-sensitive development that raises the bar in quality for our residents.

Site Context

Dancer Wood is located at 4621/4631 Pipeline Road and would be a neighbour to Saanich Commonwealth Place and the Caselton Place development.

This is a prime location for family-oriented housing. Services, parks and schools are all within easy walking or cycling distance. The nearby Royal Oak transit exchange provides easy connections to downtown and the rest of the CRD.

Neighbourhood

The site is centrally located in Royal Oak neighbourhood, with transit, parks, a rec centre, and grocerieswithin walking distance. The homes will be oriented towards seniors looking to downsize, young families, and those looking for flexible living spaces. Project Priorities

The project team has put considerable thought to best meet the needs of our community while respecting the local context and setting a precedent for a new type of housing in Saanich. Our team is comprised of local planners, engineers and designers who live and work here. Our priorities include:

  • Design: A mix of 3- and 4-bedroom strata townhouse units, each with flexible living areas with the potential for rental units. Some stacked units introduce a new form of design to Saanich.
  • Agriculture: Re-purposing an underutilized property through a swap of Agricultural Land Reserve (ALR) land will result in a net gain of agricultural land to the ALR. The resulting property will remain in the ALR where it will be adjacent to the neighbouring ALR property. Opportunities for urban farm or a partnership with a land trust are being explored.
  • Livability: with proximity to Royal Oak School, Royal Oak bus exchange, Commonwealth Place, Elk/Beaver Lake, Camosun College Interurban and the Pat Bay and Highway 1, the project is central. Working families, students, and those seeking an active lifestyle will be able to access their needs with minimal reliance on personal vehicles.
  • Active transportation: plug-in ready parking, new and improved connector trails for pedestrians and cyclists, and proximity to the Royal Oak bus exchange support active transportation rather than vehicles.
  • Affordability: through quality design and materials along with a variety of units and floor plates, the ‘missing middle’ incomes as determined by the Capital Regional District in 2019 will be able to finance and comfortably maintain these units.
  • Responsible : with demonstrated success in other projects in the CRD, we can commit to quality of construction and smart design ensuring long-term sustainability of the project and affordability for residents. This includes low ownership/strata fees due to consideration in design, materials, and landscaping.

Why Now?

Like many other places, Saanich and the Capital Regional District are facing a housing crisis. Prices have skyrocketed and many families cannot find suitable housing. Both regional and municipal policies support expanding the diversity of housing available, in order to a diversity of needs.

A townhouse development like Dancer Wood is part of the “missing middle” of housing, falling between more expensive single-family homes and less expensive, but less flexible, condo units in apartment buildings. This unique stacked townhouse project offers much greater amenities to the residents and neighbourhoods, as well as the natural environment through significant environmental improvements.

Local Environment

Normandy Creek runs along the south end of the property, and care will be taken to ensure its protection as an asset to the neighbourhood and larger watershed. The project will respect and enhance the current streamside area and exceed Saanich’s and the province’s regulations.

An innovative stormwater management program is being proposed, along with significantly enhanced agricultural soil capability.

Community Benefits

The project team recognizes not only the Saanich development requirements for development but the importance of addressing impacts to the larger neighbourhood. As such, discussions with residents and neighbourhood groups on opportunities for contributions to local priorities are welcomed and all ideas will be considered. To date, areas of focus include:

  • Attainable housing: focusing on financially attainable housing middle income households through accessible purchase price and ongoing maintenance costs.
  • Upgrades to Pipeline Road, including LED lighting and pedestrian safety
  • Active transportation connections to Caselton Place and Commonwealth Place Park
  • A net gain to the ALR on a piece of land more amenable to farming
